How This Pet Food Calculator Works

Our calculator uses modern veterinary science for pet food equations, including the Resting Energy Requirement (RER) and Maintenance Energy Requirement (MER). These formulas consider species, precise age, weight, activity, and (for dogs/cats) spay/neuter status.
For scientific references, see: AAFCO, AVMA, ASPCA, PetMD

Pet Nutrition Guidelines by Species

Dogs

Dogs are America’s #1 most popular pet, with over 65 million households owning at least one in 2024. Feeding is based on real metabolic models. Calculation formula:
RER = 70 × (weight in kg)0.75, then MER = RER × factor based on activity/age.

Cats

With 38 million US households keeping cats, proper diet is vital. Cats have unique protein/fat needs. Use our custom input for kittens, adults, and seniors for best results.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if I’m feeding my pet enough?

Monitor your pet’s body condition—if ribs are easily felt but not visible, you’re usually on track. Use our calculator for a science-based starting point, but always adjust based on your pet’s weight trend.

What is the best diet for my type of pet?

The ideal diet depends on your pet’s age, species, weight, and activity. Always choose food that meets AAFCO guidelines and consult your vet for tailored advice.

How often should I change my pet’s feeding schedule?

Major changes should be gradual, every 6–12 months unless advised by your vet due to weight or health issues.

Can I use this calculator for wet and dry food?

This tool is built for standard kibble but provides grams you can use for wet food as well. Remember, calorie density does change!

Should I feed my pet once or multiple times daily?

Multiple small meals improve digestion and satisfaction, especially for puppies, kittens, and high-energy pets. Adults may do fine with twice-daily feeding.

Can I use home-cooked diets?

Only after clear guidance from your vet or a board-certified pet nutritionist.

Should I change portions as my pet ages?

Absolutely—requirements decrease for most older pets. Younger animals and very active pets need more per pound.
